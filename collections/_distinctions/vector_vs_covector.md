vector vs. covector
* Vectors and covectors seem very similar when written in terms of coordinates. In a three-dimensional real vector space, the coordinates of a vector or covector are both written (w.r.t. some basis) as as tuple of n real numbers, such as (1, 3, -2.4) or (-0.4, 0, 102). The key point here is *with respect to some basis*. If you use a different basis, then the coordinates change, and the rules for how they change are different between vectors and covectors. Since the basis vectors are For vectors, the coordinates change in the “opposite direction” as the change in the coordinates of the basis vectors. This is easiest to see in 1-dimension.
